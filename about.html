About

How I work

Career context

I'm a UX designer based in Frederick, MD, focused on the interfaces between clinicians and AI — specifically the moment a model hands off a recommendation and a person has to decide whether to trust it. I don't have a prior design internship; what I have instead is one case study built the way I'd want a junior hire's first real project to look: research traced to sources, design decisions traced to research, and results traced to actual usability testing — not opinion.

Process

Every screen I ship answers to a specific research finding, not a hunch — you can see that traced end-to-end in the Healthcare AI case study's decision log. I run a Stark accessibility audit before any design is considered final, WCAG 2.2 AA minimum. And because I'm specializing in AI-assisted clinical workflows, I work with the tools that actually sit under the interface, not just around it — I use the Claude API directly to prototype and debug the structured data (tool schemas, extraction logic) that AI-forward products depend on, which means I can catch a mislabeled field before it becomes a UI bug instead of after.
